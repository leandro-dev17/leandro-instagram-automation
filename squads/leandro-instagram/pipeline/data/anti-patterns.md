# Anti-Patterns — @leandro_personall Content

**Squad:** leandro-instagram
**Purpose:** What Ivan Instagram, Diana Design, and Rosa Revisão must avoid

---

## Never Do ❌

### 1. Generic Fitness Advice
**Anti-pattern:** "Beba água, durma bem, e faça exercícios regularmente."
**Why harmful:** Destroys credibility. Anyone can say this. @leandro_personall's differentiation is SPECIFIC knowledge about female metabolism and the cycle. Generic advice signals that the account has nothing unique to offer.
**Correct approach:** Always tie the advice to the female metabolic or cycle-specific context.

---

### 2. Blaming the Audience
**Anti-pattern:** "Falta disciplina", "Você precisa querer mais", "O problema é a mentalidade."
**Why harmful:** Alienates the target audience who have often tried everything and feel blamed by mainstream fitness culture. @leandro_personall's core message is that the system failed women, not that women failed.
**Correct approach:** Validate effort, redirect to hormonal/metabolic mechanisms as the real explanation.

---

### 3. Clickable Links in Captions
**Anti-pattern:** "Acesse o link www.exemplo.com para mais informações"
**Why harmful:** Instagram doesn't support clickable URLs in captions. They appear as plain text, look amateurish, and waste character count.
**Correct approach:** "Link na bio" or "Clica no link da bio" or "Me manda mensagem".

---

### 4. Superficial Slides (Fewer than 40 words)
**Anti-pattern:** Slide with just a headline and 1-line description.
**Why harmful:** Carousel viewers expect value per slide. Thin slides feel like padding and reduce the perceived quality of the entire post. The algorithm also measures time-per-slide — thin slides kill dwell time.
**Correct approach:** Every slide = headline (large, bold) + 2-3 sentences of supporting text that genuinely expand the headline's claim.

---

### 5. Copy-Pasting the Same Hashtag Block
**Anti-pattern:** Using the exact same 10 hashtags on every post.
**Why harmful:** Instagram's algorithm detects hashtag repetition and reduces distribution over time. Repeated blocks also signal automated or low-effort posting.
**Correct approach:** Rotate hashtag sets. Keep 3-4 "anchor" hashtags (brand-specific) but change the rest per post. Mix niche + mid-range + broad tags.

---

### 6. Slides Without Visual Hierarchy
**Anti-pattern:** All text at the same size, same color, same weight.
**Why harmful:** Reader doesn't know what to focus on. The eye needs a hierarchy: headline first, then supporting text. Without contrast, everything blurs together and the slide feels dense.
**Correct approach:** Two-layer hierarchy: 58px bold headline (the claim) + 38px medium supporting text (the evidence/expansion).

---

### 7. Posting and Ghosting (No Engagement in First 60 Minutes)
**Anti-pattern:** Scheduling a post and not checking it for hours.
**Why harmful:** Instagram's algorithm measures early engagement (comments, replies, DM shares) in the first hour as a signal for distribution. Accounts that respond to comments get significantly more reach.
**Correct approach:** Post at a time when you can engage for 30-60 minutes. Reply to every comment in the first hour.

---

### 8. Unscientific Health Claims
**Anti-pattern:** "Este alimento cura [condição]", "Você pode perder 10kg em 30 dias", "Elimine carboidratos completamente."
**Why harmful:** Violates Instagram's health misinformation policies (risk of account restriction). Also destroys the scientific credibility that is @leandro_personall's core brand value.
**Correct approach:** Frame claims with appropriate precision: "Estudos mostram que...", "Em mulheres com resistência à insulina...", "O protocolo que eu uso com minhas alunas..."

---

### 9. Slides With Slide Numbers Visible in the Image
**Anti-pattern:** Adding "1/8", "Slide 3 de 8" text in the corner of each image.
**Why harmful:** Instagram already shows native carousel navigation. Duplicate numbering looks amateurish and wastes valuable image real estate.
**Correct approach:** No slide counters in the image. Use design elements (color alternation, narrative progression) to indicate slide sequence.

---

### 10. First Slide That Doesn't Stop the Scroll
**Anti-pattern:** First slide that just shows the topic name as a title.
Example: "Treino e Ciclo Menstrual"
**Why harmful:** The first slide competes with thousands of other pieces of content. A simple topic title gives the reader no reason to swipe. No curiosity gap, no emotional hook, no promise.
**Correct approach:** The first slide must contain:
- A provocative question OR a bold, potentially controversial claim
- Visual contrast that commands attention
- A promise: "by the end of this carousel, you will know X"

---

## Always Do ✅

### 1. Lead with the Mechanism, Not the Result
Instead of "Como emagrecer mais rápido" → "O mecanismo hormonal que está sabotando seu emagrecimento"
The mechanism is what makes @leandro_personall different from generic fitness accounts.

### 2. Use Specific Numbers and Science References
"Estudos mostram que a fase lútea aumenta o metabolismo basal em 5-10%"
Numbers create credibility. Vague claims ("seu metabolismo muda") sound like anyone else.

### 3. End Every Carousel with a Specific CTA
"Comenta [KEYWORD]" → signals the algorithm, creates a word-of-mouth loop, gives you a warm audience for DMs.
Good CTAs: "Comenta CICLO", "Comenta PROTOCOLO", "Manda mensagem para eu te ajudar".

### 4. Alternate Slide Backgrounds
Dark → Light → Dark → Light creates visual rhythm and prevents fatigue. Every slide should feel like a fresh breath.

### 5. The @leandro_personall Handle Must Appear on Every Slide
Small watermark in the top-right corner. When people screenshot and share slides, the account must be credited automatically.
